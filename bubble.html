Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu, Nepal
It was only a few days into the crisis that Jan arrived at Delta Hangar. The massive doors opened only a small crack and as soon as he and Marie had passed through, they closed again. No sunlight. Dead air.

Inside a C-5 plane like the one which brought them was sitting on blocks, its nose in the air, revealing its inner cargo bays. The plastic seals were being torn off the equipment, tents being set up under the wings.

In the buzz of activity Jan saw only one thing: a stainless steel tube with water beading down its frozen face - the containment freezer.

It's not ebola. I don't think you know what I'm telling you. It's not in your book.
